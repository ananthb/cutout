How It Works

Cutout sits between the outside world and your real email address, acting as an invisible relay.

Inbound flow (someone emails your alias)

  1. An email arrives at a1b2c3@yourdomain.com
  2. Cloudflare Email Routing triggers the Cutout worker
  3. The worker looks up the alias in D1 to find the destination address
  4. If the alias is enabled, the email is forwarded to your real address via send_email
  5. The From header is rewritten so replies go back through the alias
  6. The original sender's address is preserved in a Reply-To encoding

Outbound flow (you reply through an alias)

  1. You reply to a forwarded email from your real mailbox
  2. The reply is addressed to a special reverse-path address that encodes the original sender
  3. Cloudflare Email Routing triggers the worker again
  4. The worker decodes the original sender from the address
  5. The reply is sent via send_email with your alias as the From address
  6. The recipient sees the reply coming from a1b2c3@yourdomain.com — never your real address

Key components

  • Cloudflare Email Routing — catch-all rule that sends all inbound email to the worker
  • send_email binding — the Workers API for sending outbound email (no SMTP needed)
  • KV — stores alias-to-destination routing config
  • MIME rewriting — rewrites headers to maintain the alias illusion on both sides
