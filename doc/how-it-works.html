How It Works

Cutout sits between the outside world and your real email address, acting as an invisible relay.

Routing model

You configure an ordered list of rules at /manage. Each rule matches inbound mail with glob patterns on the local part and the domain (e.g. shop+*@example.com), and has an action:

  • Forward to one or more destination addresses, or
  • Drop silently.

Rules are evaluated top-to-bottom; the first match wins. A *@* catch-all is pinned to the end and defaults to Drop, so mail you haven't written a rule for is discarded rather than accidentally leaked.

Inbound flow (someone emails one of your addresses)

  1. An email arrives at an address on any domain pointed at Cutout (e.g. newsletter@yourdomain.com)
  2. Cloudflare Email Routing's catch-all rule triggers the Cutout worker
  3. The worker matches the recipient against your rules, top-to-bottom
  4. On a Forward match, for each verified destination the worker:
    • Generates a one-time reverse-alias reply+<uuid>@yourdomain.com
    • Stores a mapping reply+<uuid> → original sender in KV with a 30-day TTL
    • Rewrites From and Reply-To to that reverse-alias, preserves the body/subject/threading headers
    • Sends the rewritten message via the send_email binding
  5. Unverified destinations are silently skipped (see verification below)

Reply flow (you reply through a reverse-alias)

  1. You reply from your real mailbox; the reply is addressed to the reply+<uuid>@yourdomain.com that Cutout put on the forwarded message
  2. Email Routing triggers the worker again
  3. The worker looks up reply+<uuid> in KV to find the original sender
  4. The reply is re-sent via send_email with your original inbound address (e.g. newsletter@yourdomain.com) as the From
  5. The recipient sees the reply coming from your public alias — never your real mailbox

Destination verification

Cloudflare Email Service will deliver to any address, so Cutout does its own check to prove the rule author actually controls a destination:

  1. When you add a Forward rule, Cutout sends a one-time link from verify@<cutout-host> to each unverified destination
  2. The link hits the public route /verify/<token> (outside Cloudflare Access)
  3. Clicking confirms the destination; it's then stored in KV under verified:<email> and usable in any rule
  4. Until confirmed, mail matching a rule that forwards only to unverified destinations is dropped silently
  5. The /manage UI shows a ✓ for verified destinations and a resend button for unverified ones

Key components

  • Cloudflare Email Routing — inbound: catch-all → worker, one per zone
  • Cloudflare Email Service — outbound: the same send_email binding, but with onboarded sender domains it can deliver to any recipient (no per-address verification at the Cloudflare level)
  • KV — stores the ordered rule list, reverse-alias mappings (30-day TTL), verified destinations, and pending verification tokens (24-hour TTL)
  • Cloudflare Access — protects /manage only; /verify/<token> and /health are public
  • Structured outbound — incoming MIME is parsed into subject/text/html/headers and re-sent via Email Service's structured API rather than re-serialised. Threading headers (In-Reply-To, References) and the loop-detection header are passed through; exotic MIME structures and attachments are not preserved.

Multiple domains

One worker can serve any number of zones. For each email domain, enable Email Routing and point its catch-all at the same Cutout worker; rules use the domain glob to differentiate them. Reverse-aliases and verifications are derived from the domain the mail actually arrived on.
